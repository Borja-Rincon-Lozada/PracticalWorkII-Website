A clean hub for the subject: a quick description, the units (PDFs), and the practical assignments. No left sidebar, just direct access.

Description of the subject

Fundamentals of Computer Engineering (FCE) is a 6-ECTS, first-year, first-semester basic training course that provides a global view of Computer Engineering from both an academic and professional perspective. It introduces the foundations behind the discipline (including mathematics, physics, anthropology, and ethics) and helps students understand the social impact and real role of computer engineers today. The course is structured in two parts: (1) Theoretical Foundations (history and context of ICT, hardware/software, operating systems, networks, emerging trends, and data/big-data fundamentals with legal/ethical aspects) and (2) Practical Skills, an introduction to web development, including Git/GitHub, HTML5, and CSS3, with an expected workload of about 150 hours. This appears in the course guide itself.
