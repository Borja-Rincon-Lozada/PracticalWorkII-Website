Agriculture Robotics

In this practical work we explore in depth the use of robotics in agriculture as well as its possible future.

Agriculture Robotics AI IoT

Overview

Agriculture robotics refers to automated machines used to perform farming tasks, such as planting, harvesting, and monitoring crops, often with minimal human intervention.

1) Objectives
  • Analyse the current and future role of agricultural robots in the automation of farm work.
  • Describe what an agricultural robot is and describe its main types.
  • Evaluate the benefits and limitations of their use compared to traditional methods.
  • Analyze future trends, especially the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and the Internet of Things (IoT).
  • Reflect on the ethical, social, and environmental consequences of using robots in agriculture.
2) Description
A robot is a machine capable of acting on its own through three essential components: sensors, actuators, and programs. An agricultural robot is “any robotic device that can improve agricultural processes, by taking over many of the farmer’s duties that are slow or labour intensive.”
3) Different types
  • Planting and seeding robots
  • Harvesting and picking robots
  • Drones
  • Crop sprayers
  • Weeding and mowing machines
  • Sorting and grading robots
  • Autonomous tractors
  • Livestock management robots
4) Advantages and limitations
  • Increased efficiency and productivity: operate continuously (24/7) without needing breaks.
  • Precision and consistency: apply fertilisers or pesticides only where needed.
  • Enhanced sustainability: optimising resource usage and reduce the environmental impact of agriculture.
  • Maintenance is complicated and expensive: regular maintenance, professional servicing and spare parts are costly.
  • Potential job losses: risk of reduced employment opportunities within farming and rural communities.
5) Ethical view
  • Centralization of the power
  • Data and privacy of the farmers
  • Cybersecurity Concerns
  • Sustainability
  • Environmental Impact
  • Job displacement
